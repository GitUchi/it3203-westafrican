Staple Foods of West Africa

The backbone of West African cuisine is built on a handful of remarkable ingredients — hearty, versatile, and deeply tied to the land and history of the region.

Jollof Rice

Jollof Rice is arguably the most famous dish in all of West Africa. It is a one-pot rice dish cooked in a rich tomato and pepper base, seasoned with onions, garlic, and a blend of spices including thyme, bay leaf, and stock. Every country has its own version — the great "Jollof Wars" between Nigeria and Ghana is one of the most spirited culinary debates in the world.

The dish traces its origins to the Wolof people of Senegal and Gambia, where it was originally known as thieboudienne — rice cooked in fish broth.

Fufu

Fufu is a smooth, dough-like staple made by boiling and pounding starchy vegetables — typically cassava, yam, or plantain — until they reach a stretchy, elastic consistency.

Fufu is not chewed — it is swallowed. This is considered proper eating etiquette in many West African cultures.

Types of Fufu by Country

  • Nigeria: Cassava fufu (also called akpu), pounded yam
  • Ghana: Cassava and plantain fufu
  • Senegal / Mali: Millet-based tô
  • Côte d'Ivoire: Yam-based foutou

Plantains

Plantains are a starchy cousin of the banana and one of the most versatile ingredients in West African cooking. Unlike sweet bananas, plantains must be cooked before eating.

How Plantains Are Prepared

  • Dodo (Fried Ripe Plantain): Sweet, caramelized, and slightly crispy — a beloved side dish in Nigeria
  • Kelewele (Spiced Fried Plantain): A Ghanaian street food seasoned with ginger, pepper, and spices
  • Boiled Plantain: Served alongside soups or grilled fish
  • Unripe Plantain Porridge: Cooked with palm oil, pepper, and crayfish

Yam

Yam is considered sacred in many West African cultures, particularly in Nigeria and Ghana where the New Yam Festival marks the annual harvest season.

Yam is boiled, fried, roasted, or pounded into fufu. Yam pepper soup, yam porridge (asaro), and fried yam with eggs are everyday household staples across the region.
