A Journey Through West African Cuisine

West African cuisine is one of the most diverse, flavorful, and historically significant culinary traditions on earth. Rooted in centuries of agriculture, trade, and cultural exchange, these foods tell the story of entire civilizations. This site explores the ingredients, dishes, and traditions that define the region's extraordinary food culture.

About This Research

This website presents research on the traditional foods of West Africa — a region encompassing countries such as Nigeria, Ghana, Senegal, Mali, Guinea, Côte d'Ivoire, and more. The research examines staple ingredients, iconic dishes, cooking techniques, and the cultural significance of food in West African society.

West African food has also had a profound global impact. The transatlantic slave trade carried West African culinary traditions to the Americas, where they became the foundation of Southern American, Caribbean, and Brazilian food cultures. Understanding West African cuisine is understanding a global culinary story.

"Food is not just sustenance in West Africa — it is a language of community, identity, and celebration."

Quick Facts: West African Food Culture

Fact Detail
Number of countries in West Africa 16 officially recognized nations
Most widely eaten staple Rice, yam, cassava, and millet depending on subregion
Most iconic dish internationally Jollof Rice (debated between Nigeria, Ghana, and Senegal)
Key cooking fat Palm oil — used across nearly all West African cuisines
Global influence Foundation of Caribbean, Soul Food, and Brazilian cuisine
Primary protein sources Fish, goat, chicken, beans, and groundnuts (peanuts)
