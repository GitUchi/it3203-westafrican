Soups & Stews

West African soups and stews are slow-cooked, deeply layered, and built around bold flavors. They are the counterpart to staple starches — always eaten together, never apart.

In West Africa, a meal is rarely a dish alone — it is a starch paired with a soup or stew. These soups are not thin broths. They are thick, rich, intensely flavored preparations that take hours to develop. They form the emotional and nutritional center of the West African table.

Egusi Soup

Egusi soup is made from ground melon seeds (egusi) cooked in palm oil with leafy greens, meat or fish, and a base of tomatoes and peppers. The ground seeds create a thick, grainy texture that absorbs the flavors of everything around it. It is one of the most widely eaten soups in Nigeria, Ghana, and Cameroon.

Egusi Soup — rich, earthy, and deeply satisfying

Typical Ingredients

  • Ground egusi (melon seeds)
  • Palm oil
  • Stockfish or smoked fish
  • Meat (goat, beef, or tripe)
  • Bitter leaf, spinach, or ugu (fluted pumpkin leaves)
  • Crayfish (dried and ground)
  • Scotch bonnet pepper and tomatoes
  • Seasoning cubes and salt
Egusi seeds are protein-rich — about 30% protein by weight — making this soup one of the most nutritionally complete dishes in West African cuisine.

Groundnut Soup (Peanut Stew)

Groundnut soup — known as maafe in Senegal and Mali, and nkate nkwan in Ghana — is a rich peanut-based stew slow-cooked with tomatoes, onions, hot peppers, and meat.

The dish traveled across the Atlantic with enslaved West Africans and became the ancestor of peanut-based stews found throughout the American South and the Caribbean.

Okra Soup

Okra soup uses sliced or blended okra as its thickening agent. When cooked, okra releases a natural mucilage that gives the soup a distinctive slippery, draw texture — which is prized in West African cooking.

Why Okra Matters

  • Okra (Abelmoschus esculentus) is native to West Africa
  • It was carried to the Americas by enslaved West Africans
  • The word "gumbo" in Louisiana Creole cuisine comes from ki ngombo, a Bantu word for okra
  • Rich in fiber, vitamins C and K, and antioxidants

Pepper Soup

Pepper soup is a light, intensely spiced broth made with meat or fish, a unique blend of West African spices (including uziza seeds, ehuru, and uda), and scotch bonnet peppers.

Medicinal Use Spiced Broth Nigerian Cameroonian

In Nigeria, pepper soup is a staple at beer parlors and outdoor restaurants — always served piping hot in a clay pot or deep bowl.
